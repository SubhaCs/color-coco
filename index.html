Colorizing grayscale images featuring diverse objects presents a formidable challenge for many image colorization algorithms, especially when confronted with complex real-world scenes. Traditional methods often struggle to produce coherent color outputs due to the inherent ambiguity of colorization, where multiple color possibilities exist for each grayscale input. In this study, we introduce a novel text-guided colorization approach that integrates textual descriptions of scene objects as additional input to grayscale images, thus improving the accuracy of the colorization process. Our colorization method employs a deep network that takes two inputs: the grayscale image and its corresponding coarse-encoded textual description. By training the network to understand the relationship between the image and its textual description, we predict the relevant color components. To achieve this, we employ a multi-modal feature attention mechanism that facilitates colorization of detected objects through the Instance Object Colorization (IOC) module. This module is conditioned on both the grayscale image and its associated language description. Diverging from conventional colorization techniques that rely solely on unconditional generation from grayscale inputs, our approach leverages the fusion of image and text to produce more precise and consistent colorization results. Additionally, as part of this study, we curate a dataset using the COCO dataset, comprising textual descriptions of the color information for each object in the images. This dataset enhances our understanding of object-color relationships and facilitates training and evaluation of our text-guided colorization model.
